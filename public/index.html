Router Tips and Tricks

  • As you’ve probably found out already, router companies do not make it easy to know which is the right router for you
  • You’ll never get the theoretical speed that you pay for from your internet service provider. There is “overhead” which eats into the actual speed you receive. Using a wifi signal vs wired connection will slow the connection down even more. If you pay for 1gb speed. You’ll most likely receive 700mbps maximum from the wall. And 500 mbps maximum over a 5ghz wireless network that is close to your router without any obstructions.
  • Anything that is in the line of sight of your router will slow down your potential speed
  • 5ghz is a faster frequency than 2.4 ghz, but 2.4 ghz has a longer range since it can penetrate through more objects
  • Speeds listed by the wifi router companies are again theoretical. They also like to list the combined total speed across all frequencies which conflates the actual speed you could receive. (You’ll never get 2,300 mbps)
  • Many devices are not compatible to receive fast internet speeds. My Vizio TV only receives about 32mbps when wired connected when my laptop receives 500+mbps over wifi. That’s just because it’s an older TV.
  • Expectation setting - anything over 100mbps is really fast and you should be happy!
  • You’ll most likely not be able to use Time Machine over your wifi router USB connected external — sad I know.
  • The number of antennas does not necessarily mean more range.
  • MIMO is a great technology that is the best thing you need if you have a bunch of devices .
  • QOS (Quality of Service) isn’t needed if you have speeds above 300mbps.
